# Time-Series Forecasting: Pattern-Level Production Judgment

**Version 1.0 — 2026-07-06. Extended tier: production patterns + common pitfalls.** Standalone. Related: [../principles/data-leakage.md](../principles/data-leakage.md) (time-series is where leakage lives), [../principles/evaluation.md](../principles/evaluation.md) §2 (time-based splitting — mandatory here, not optional), [../principles/monitoring-and-drift.md](../principles/monitoring-and-drift.md).

---

Forecasting is ML with the future as the label — which makes it the domain where every leakage and evaluation rule in this KB is load-bearing on every single line. If a forecasting system's offline numbers look great and production disappoints, the cause is almost always one of the temporal leaks below; I'd estimate half the forecasting systems I've audited had at least one.

## Production patterns

- **Beat the dumb baseline or ship the dumb baseline.** Seasonal-naive (same hour last week / same day last year) and last-value carry-forward are brutally strong on real business series. Every model reports skill relative to them (e.g., MASE, which is scale-free and baseline-relative by construction); a deep model that beats seasonal-naive by 3% may still lose to it on the volatile 10% of series that matter. Many production "forecasting platforms" are, correctly, seasonal-naive plus holiday adjustments plus a model for the head series.
- **Backtesting = rolling-origin evaluation, simulating the production cadence** ([../principles/evaluation.md](../principles/evaluation.md) §2's retraining-realism, which forecasting makes mandatory): train up to origin T, forecast T+1..T+h, roll forward, repeat across many origins; report the *distribution* across origins and horizons, not one split's mean. One origin = one weather sample. Report error *by horizon* (h=1 vs h=14 are different products) and by series segment (head/tail volume, new/mature series).
- **Global models over per-series models as the modern default** (LightGBM/deep nets trained across all series with series-ID features, vs. one ARIMA per series): they pool strength across related series, handle cold-start series, and ship as *one* artifact — but they inherit group-leakage obligations (below) and need per-segment slicing since one global model can quietly sacrifice the tail.
- **Forecast the distribution, not the point,** when downstream decisions are asymmetric (inventory: under-stock ≠ over-stock — [../principles/evaluation.md](../principles/evaluation.md) §1's cost-matrix rule): quantile loss / pinball-evaluated prediction intervals, with the decision layer consuming the relevant quantile (service-level → P90) rather than the mean. Calibration of intervals (empirical coverage vs. nominal) is a monitored production metric.
- **Known-future covariates are the legitimate exception** to "no future data": planned promotions, prices, holidays, weather *forecasts* (not actuals — see pitfalls). Model architecture and feature pipeline must distinguish known-future inputs from observed-past inputs explicitly.

## Common pitfalls (the temporal-leakage catalog)

- **Feature windows touching the label window:** `rolling_mean_7d` computed *including* the forecast target's period, lag features off-by-one (lag-0 = the answer), or aggregates centered instead of trailing. Detector: single-feature ablation ([../principles/data-leakage.md](../principles/data-leakage.md) §1) — a lag feature that alone nails the target is off-by-one until proven otherwise.
- **Normalization/detrending fit on the full series** — scaler statistics computed through the test period leak the future's level into the past ([../principles/data-leakage.md](../principles/data-leakage.md) §3, temporal edition). All fitted transforms fit on the train window only, per rolling origin. Same for hyperparameters tuned on the final period then "validated" on it.
- **Weather/covariate actuals in place of forecasts:** trained on actual temperature, served with forecast temperature — a train/serve skew ([../principles/train-serve-skew.md](../principles/train-serve-skew.md)) that flatters offline results by exactly the weather-forecast error. Train on *archived forecasts as-of the prediction time* (vendors provide them; if not, degrade the actuals with realistic error). This one bug has invalidated entire energy-forecasting programs.
- **Data-arrival lag ignored:** the warehouse has yesterday's sales by 6am *usually* — but the model trained on complete days and serves on whatever has arrived (late-posting stores, settlement delays). Train with availability-time semantics ([../principles/train-serve-skew.md](../principles/train-serve-skew.md) §2): features as they would have looked at forecast time, including the holes.
- **Random CV on series data:** still seen constantly, still fatal — random folds put the future in training ([../principles/evaluation.md](../principles/evaluation.md) §2). Also its subtler sibling: rolling-origin *within* series but random split *across* series for a global model, when related series co-move (stores in one region share weather/promos) — a group leak through correlation; split related groups together or embargo across the boundary.
- **Retrain-and-forget on regime change:** COVID-shaped shocks make the recent past unrepresentative; scheduled retraining faithfully learns the anomaly into next year's seasonality baseline. Regime handling is a *decision*, not a default: exclusion windows, intervention dummies, or down-weighting — chosen, documented, and revisited ([../principles/monitoring-and-drift.md](../principles/monitoring-and-drift.md) §1's diagnose-before-retraining, concept-drift branch).
- **Hierarchies that don't add up:** SKU forecasts summing to ≠ store forecast ≠ region forecast, and downstream consumers reconciling ad-hoc in three different spreadsheets. If consumers need coherence, reconcile in the pipeline (bottom-up or MinT-style) and publish *one* coherent set.
- **Evaluation on revised data:** many business series get restated (returns, late bookings); evaluating January's forecast against March's restated January actuals measures against information that didn't exist. Freeze actuals-as-of evaluation snapshots ([../principles/monitoring-and-drift.md](../principles/monitoring-and-drift.md) §2's label-maturity windows are the same idea).
